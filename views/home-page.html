The greatest scientist in the history of mankind

Who was him?

Was an English scientist who made important contributions to the fields of electrochemistry and electromagnetism. The concepts underlying electromagnetic induction, diamagnetism, and electrolysis were among his most important discoveries.

What makes him the greatest?

He basically gave the foundation of the Electromagnetic Theory with no formal education at all (which is impressive).
In other words, he is the Father of Electricity.

Theory of what!?

The Electromagnetic Theory stablishes the basic principles of electrical and electronic circuits over the entire frequency spectrum from dc to optics.
In simplest words, it explains the relationship between magnetism and electricity, without it, we wouldn't telecomunications (internet is part of it), cellphones, computers... You know, all the digital era wouldn't exist.
